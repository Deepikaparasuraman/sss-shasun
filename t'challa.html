BLACK PANTHER

T'Challa is a fictional character portrayed by Chadwick Boseman in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) media franchise—based on the Marvel Comics character of the same name—commonly known by his appointed of Black Panther. He is depicted as the king of the fictional African nation of Wakanda.
He initially came into conflict with some of the Avengers but later allies with them against Thanos. T'Challa's first appeared in Captain America: Civil War (2016), and appeared in four films, making his final live action appearance in Avengers: Endgame (2019).
After Boseman died of colon cancer in August 2020, Kevin Feige confirmed that the character would not be recast nor would a digital double be used, and he will not appear in Black Panther: Wakanda Forever (2022).Instead, T'Challa was confirmed to have died prior to the events of Wakanda Forever.
Boseman's performance as T'Challa is notable as one of the first black superheroes in a big-budget film, receiving critical acclaim, and his titular film became the ninth-highest-grossing film of all time.
